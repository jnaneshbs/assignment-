Challenges and Opportunities: While we witness great excitement and rapid growth in the EV segment in the country with an increasing number of E-vehicles on the roads, it is just the beginning and a long way to go before we can claim that we have built a matured and self-reliant ecosystem in the country.
A secure raw material supply for batteries and an optimised supply chain setup are vital for the EV transition. To reach India’s ambitious domestic cell manufacturing capacity target, say 100 GWh by 2030, it would require about Lithium-10 kt, Nickel-30 kt,Cobalt-5 kt,and Graphite-110 kt (depending on technology), which are not available in India. So, we should have a mechanism to access these materials through the government’s G-G initiatives.
